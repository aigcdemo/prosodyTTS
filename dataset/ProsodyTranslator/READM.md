we collected and curated data from three distinct scenarios—live streaming, TV dramas, and lectures—each exhibiting unique prosodic characteristics. Specifically, live streaming speech is characterized by fast, variable pacing and exaggerated tones driven by real-time interaction; TV dramas display deliberate, dramatic rhythms and expressive intonation tailored to character and plot; while lectures feature steady, clear pacing and structured prosody optimized for information delivery. Since prosodic markers are extracted directly from audio, and because open‑source speech corpora contain diverse speaker styles (including non‑professional speakers whose delivery may be disfluent, leading to mismatched or unnatural semantic pauses), we used Qwen3-14B to annotate the corresponding text with semantically informed punctuation. This step improves alignment between linguistic content and prosodic labels by ensuring that punctuation reflects actual phrasing and rhythm. By integrating speech‑text alignment information, we then filtered approximately 210,000 instances in which acoustic pauses consistently matched these refined semantic pauses. The resulting high‑quality dataset was split into training, validation, and test sets in a 7:2:1 ratio.
